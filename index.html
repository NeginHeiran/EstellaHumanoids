Estella: A Mirror of Us

Estella is an open-source humanoid robot inspired by human anatomy, created to reflect the beauty of our gestures, the strength of our spine, and the freedom of our movement. Built for the community, by the community, to become a true mirror of us.

Features

Modular and Extensible Design

Each component can be replaced or upgraded independently

Human-inspired mechanism

Realistic spine, expressive face, and articulated shoulders

Open-source 3D models

CAD files (STEP, STL), and renders for easy access

Community-driven development

everyone can contribute, learn, and iterate

Current Modules

Face

- Powered by 35 independent motors for highly detailed replication of human gestures and expressions.
- Supports common emotions like smile, sadness, anger as well as subtle micro-expressions such as crooked mouth, bulging eyes, asymmetries.
- Serves as a platform for studying emotional robotics and expressive human-robot interaction.

Neck

- A dedicated parallel mechanism for neck articulation, separated from the spine for improved modularity.
- Provides smooth pitch, yaw, and roll motion to mimic natural human head movement.
- When integrated with the spine module, the neck completes the natural S-shape of the human spine, ensuring anatomically accurate posture and movement.

Spine

- Built of three 3-RSS parallel robots arranged in series to replicate the natural S-shape of the human spine, synced with neck.
- Provides realistic flexibility for torso and head posture.
- Designed for future modular integration with the pelvis and lower body.
